About

Since a young age, I’ve always been involved with some concept of development. I would draft up plans for iPods that could play both music and videos, draw out sample cards for the next big trading card game, and design blueprints to build the first hovercraft, which Elon Musk and I would now mutually agree is unfeasible.

As I got older, my version of development naturally translated into entrepreneurship. I became a semi-finalist in Foothill’s Business Plan Competition, worked on SaaS projects with friends and received formal training in business at UCR and Enterprise’s Management Training Program. After recognition as one of the top managers and sales reps in the region and consistent monthly branch profit increases, I felt it time to switch gears and enter the field of programming - something I’ve always had an interest in since high school.

Therefore, with the aspiration of pairing my enthusiasm for programming with my background in business, I joined the Actualize Programming Boot Camp & Apprenticeship to learn the much-needed hard skills. It was here that I learned not only the proper languages and frameworks for Full-Stack Development, but what it took to think as a legitimate programmer.

I will always keep my entrepreneurial interests active as a way to maintain my creativity and drive my desire to keep learning new technologies. However, my biggest focus now is to build my career in programming and find a company that provides serious mentorship where I'll be working with an engaged team to develop something fun, innovative, and exciting.

In summary, I wish to utilize my understanding of sales to drive company growth by integrating creative and lucrative product features with code built for scalability.
